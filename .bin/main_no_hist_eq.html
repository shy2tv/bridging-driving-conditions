nuScene Clear to Rain, Night, and Fog using DA-UNIT

AIM-106

Welcome to our demo page showcasing DA-UNIT for the nuScene dataset. Our goal is to transform clear-weather images into different conditions—rain, night, and fog—so that autonomous perception systems can train and evaluate their robustness in varied real-world scenarios.

DA-UNIT Model Overview

The figure below illustrates the DA-UNIT architecture used to adapt clear images to various target domains. By combining data-driven learning with auxiliary inputs (such as depth or semantic labels), the model can generate realistic weather conditions while preserving crucial scene information.

Model Background & Enhancements

Our work builds on the base model presented in the paper "Domain Adaptation of Synthetic Driving Datasets for Real-World Autonomous Driving" by Koustav Mullick, Harshil Jain, Sanchit Gupta, and Amit Arvind Kale. This paper lays the foundation for our approach.

In our implementation, we extend the base model by incorporating additional auxiliary inputs—such as depth maps, semantic segmentation, and other sensor data—to provide richer contextual information for the transformation process. Additionally, we introduce modifications to the network architecture and training procedures to enhance performance under challenging conditions such as rain, night, and fog. These improvements are detailed in our work, which is currently under review at ICML. More details can be found in our Feber report.

The results presented here are inferred using our model with only depth as an auxiliary input. The model was trained on a combination of CARLA simulated images (paired clear and adverse condition images) mixed with 10% ACDC dataset images (clear condition).

Postprocessing Details

Please note that the images displayed on this page are shown without any post processing. For a version with enhanced brightness consistency, we also offer a page where we apply a histogram equalization adjustment. In that version, we compute the average histogram of the L channel (Lightness) over the 6 images (one from each camera) in the LAB color space and use it to equalize the brightness across all images.
